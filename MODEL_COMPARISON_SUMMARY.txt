  → Example: Creative text generation, dialogue, novel writing

2. Use MiniGPT for MAIN PREDICTIONS

3. Ensemble:

THEORETICAL INSIGHTS
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Why does trigram beat complex models?

1. OCCAM'S RAZOR
   "When you have 1M tokens, contexts beyond 4 chars are sparse"
   → Simple model at limit of data = beats complex model
   → 1,360 observable contexts > 46,224 (many unseen)

2. BIAS-VARIANCE BALANCE
   Trigram = high bias, low variance = good for small data
   5-gram = low bias, HIGH variance = overfitting
   Neural = low bias, medium variance = needs regularization

3. CONTEXT WINDOW TRADE-OFF
   Trigram: P(e | "th") = can memorize all "th" occurrences
   5-gram: P(?) → too sparse, mostly prior
   Neural: learns that "th" appears in context-dependent ways
           → better for long sequences but less certain

4. FUNDAMENTAL RESULT
   "Best model depends on data size"
   Insufficient data: simple >> complex
   Sufficient data: complex >> simple
   
   Shakespeare (1M tokens):
   - Trigram has right complexity
   - 5-gram has too many parameters (46K contexts)
   - MiniGPT compensated with strong inductive bias (attention)
